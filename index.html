The Early History of Sumerian Cats

We all love cats, but what about those cats roaming in the city of Girsu? What about cats that know how design algorithms?

While not as famously deified as they were in ancient Egypt, cats in Sumer still held a special place in day-to-day life. They protected grain supplies from rodents and became symbols of grace and poise in numerous Sumerian artifacts. In clay tablets and early cuneiform scripts, there are mentions of temple cats dedicated to certain deities known for fertility and agriculture.

Sumerian Mythology & Feline Symbolism

Within the pantheon of Sumerian gods and goddesses, some held dominion over nature and creatures of the land. Cats, being agile and elusive, were sometimes associated with mysticism and protection. Legends suggest that certain temple guardians were guided by the spirit of these cats, which were believed to keep evil spirits at bay.

Although the records on direct cat worship remain sparse, references to protective feline spirits appear in myths surrounding fertility rites and harvest celebrations. Several cuneiform tablets depict cats alongside wheat sheaves or near altars, suggesting their vital role in safeguarding the livelihood of the Sumerian people.
